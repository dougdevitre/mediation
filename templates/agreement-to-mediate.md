## 1. Nature of Mediation

Mediation is a voluntary process in which an impartial mediator facilitates communication and negotiation to help the parties reach their own agreements. The mediator does not act as a judge, arbitrator, attorney, or therapist for either party. The mediator will not make decisions for the parties or pressure them to reach an agreement.

## 2. Self-Determination

Each party has the right to make their own decisions about whether to participate in mediation, how the process is structured, and whether to agree to any proposals. Each party may withdraw from mediation at any time and for any reason.

## 3. Impartiality

The mediator will remain impartial throughout the process and will not favor either party. If the mediator becomes aware of any conflict of interest or inability to remain impartial, the mediator will disclose this to the parties and, if necessary, withdraw.

## 4. Confidentiality

All communications, documents, and information shared during mediation are confidential, except as follows:
- Information the parties agree to disclose
- Information required to be disclosed by law (including mandatory reporting of child maltreatment and threats of harm)
- Information required by court order
- A signed mediation agreement (which is not confidential)

Separate sessions (caucus): Information shared with the mediator during separate sessions will not be disclosed to the other party without the disclosing party's express permission.

## 5. No Legal Advice

The mediator will not provide legal advice to either party. Each party is strongly encouraged to consult with an independent attorney before, during, and after the mediation process. Each party is encouraged to have any agreement reviewed by an independent attorney before signing.

## 6. Attorneys and Support Persons

Each party may have an attorney, advocate, or support person present during mediation sessions. The parties agree to inform the mediator in advance of any additional participants.

## 7. Good Faith Participation

Each party agrees to participate in good faith, provide accurate and complete information, and disclose all relevant financial and other information necessary for informed decision-making.

## 8. Technology and Online Sessions

If mediation sessions are conducted using technology (videoconference, phone, or other platform):
- Each party consents to the use of [Platform Name] for sessions
- Each party agrees to participate from a private location
- No party shall record sessions without the written consent of all parties and the mediator
- The mediator will use reasonable measures to protect the security of digital communications

## 9. Children

The parties agree to focus on the best interests of their children in all discussions and decisions. The mediator may raise issues related to children's wellbeing, safety, and developmental needs.

## 11. Mediator's Right to Suspend or Terminate

The mediator may suspend or terminate mediation if the mediator believes that:
- A party is unable or unwilling to meaningfully participate
- The safety of a party or child cannot be ensured
- The mediation process is being misused
- The mediator's impartiality has been compromised
- Continuing mediation would be inappropriate for any other compelling reason

## 12. Agreement Not to Subpoena

The parties agree not to subpoena or otherwise compel the mediator to testify or produce documents in any legal proceeding related to this matter, to the extent permitted by law.

## 13. Enforceability of Agreements

Any agreement reached in mediation will not be binding until reviewed by each party's attorney (if desired), signed by the parties, and, if required, approved by the court.

## 14. Court Approval

If court approval of any mediated agreement is required by law, the parties understand that the agreement must be submitted to and approved by the court before it becomes enforceable.
