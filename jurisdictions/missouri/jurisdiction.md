# Missouri Jurisdiction Module

> How the 2025 AFCC/ABA Model Standards interact with Missouri law. This is educational information, not legal advice. Verify all citations against current statutes — laws change.

---

## Missouri Mediation Statutes

### RSMo 452.372 — Mediation in Custody and Visitation

Missouri courts may order mediation in contested custody and visitation cases. Key provisions:

- Courts may order mediation at any time during proceedings
- Mediation costs may be allocated between parties by the court
- The mediator shall assess the appropriateness of mediation in each case, considering domestic violence
- If domestic violence is alleged, the court shall not order mediation unless the victim requests it and the court finds adequate safeguards can be implemented
- Mediator qualifications are set by local court rules

### RSMo 452.375 — Best Interests of the Child

Missouri uses the "best interests of the child" standard for custody determinations. Relevant factors include the child's wishes, adjustment to home/school/community, mental and physical health of all parties, and the willingness of each parent to facilitate the child's relationship with the other parent.

### RSMo 452.400 — Parenting Plans

Missouri requires a parenting plan in all custody cases. The plan must include a custody arrangement, visitation schedule, and provisions for decision-making. Mediators should be familiar with the required elements.

## Confidentiality in Missouri

### RSMo 435.014 — Uniform Mediation Act (Partial Adoption)

Missouri has adopted portions of the Uniform Mediation Act. Key confidentiality provisions:

- Mediation communications are generally privileged
- Exceptions include: signed mediated agreements, threats of bodily harm, information used to plan or commit a crime, professional malpractice claims

### Court Rules

- Missouri Circuit Courts may have local rules governing mediation confidentiality
- Court-connected mediation programs typically have their own confidentiality policies
- The mediator should provide parties with written information about the specific confidentiality protections that apply

## Mandatory Reporting in Missouri

### RSMo 210.115 — Mandatory Reporters of Child Abuse/Neglect

Missouri has a broad mandatory reporting statute. Key points for mediators:

**Who must report**: Physicians, dentists, nurses, hospital workers, psychologists, mental health professionals, social workers, day care workers, teachers, school officials, law enforcement, ministers, and "other persons with responsibility for the care of children."

**Important for mediators**: Whether mediators fall under "other persons with responsibility for the care of children" may depend on the mediator's professional license and the specific context. Mediators who are also licensed attorneys, therapists, social workers, or counselors are typically mandatory reporters under their professional license.

**What to report**: Known or suspected abuse or neglect of a child.

**Where to report**: Missouri Children's Division (CD) hotline: 1-800-392-3738

**Immunity**: Persons who report in good faith are immune from civil and criminal liability.

> **Practice note**: Even if a mediator is not technically a mandatory reporter under Missouri law, the 2025 Model Standards strongly encourage mediators to act as if they are mandatory reporters when child maltreatment is identified.

## Domestic Violence and Mediation in Missouri

### RSMo 452.372(3) — Domestic Violence Safeguards

When domestic violence is alleged in a custody case:
- The court shall not order mediation unless the victim requests it
- The court must find that adequate safeguards can be implemented
- Safeguards may include separate sessions, staggered scheduling, and other protections

### Full Orders of Protection — RSMo Chapter 455

Mediators should check whether any active orders of protection exist between the parties. An order of protection may affect whether and how mediation can be conducted.

## Mediator Qualifications in Missouri

Missouri does not have a statewide mediator certification program. Qualifications are typically set by:

- Local circuit court rules for court-connected mediators
- The Missouri Bar (for attorney-mediators)
- Professional organizations (AFCC, ACR, APFM)

### Recommended Minimum Training (per 2025 Standards)

- 40+ hours of mediation training (some Missouri circuits require this for court panels)
- Specific training in domestic abuse screening and dynamics
- Specific training in child maltreatment recognition
- Family law substantive knowledge
- Continuing education annually
