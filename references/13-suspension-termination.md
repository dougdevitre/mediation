# 13 — Suspension & Termination

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard XII (Suspension & Termination); 2005 General Standard I(A), VI (Quality)

## Purpose

Define when and how a mediator should suspend or terminate the mediation process, balancing party self-determination with safety and process integrity.

---

## Grounds for Suspension or Termination

### Mandatory Grounds (SHALL)
- A party is unable to meaningfully participate
- Safety of a party or child cannot be ensured
- Mediator's impartiality is compromised
- Mediator discovers a conflict of interest that cannot be waived

### Discretionary Grounds (SHOULD / MAY)
- Domestic abuse identified and barriers cannot be remediated
- Child maltreatment alleged and mediator lacks required training
- A party is acting in bad faith
- Mediation is being used to harass, delay, or manipulate
- An impasse that cannot be resolved
- A party needs additional information or expert consultation
- A party needs time to obtain legal counsel
- Substance use is impairing a party's decision-making during sessions
- The mediator reasonably believes the process is no longer productive

---

## Suspension vs. Termination

| Action | When | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Suspension** | Temporary barrier; situation may change; party needs time | Mediation pauses; can resume when conditions improve |
| **Termination** | Barrier is permanent; safety cannot be ensured; process integrity compromised | Mediation ends; parties referred to alternative processes |

---

## Termination Protocol

1. If possible, meet with each party separately to explain the decision
2. Be transparent about the general reason (without violating confidentiality of the other party's disclosures)
3. Do not assign blame or characterize either party's behavior in a way that could be used in court
4. Provide referrals to appropriate alternative processes, legal counsel, or support services
5. Confirm the scope of ongoing confidentiality obligations
6. Document the termination in case records (within confidentiality limits)
7. Send written notice if appropriate (→ templates/termination-notice.md)

---

## What NOT to Say or Document

- Do not state which party "caused" the termination
- Do not characterize either party as abusive, uncooperative, or mentally unfit
- Do not make recommendations about custody, parenting, or legal outcomes
- Do not provide information that could be used against either party in court
- Keep the termination notice neutral and process-focused
