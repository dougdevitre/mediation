# 16 — Advertising & Marketing Compliance

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard XIV; 2005 General Standard VII (Advertising & Solicitation)

## Purpose

Ensure truthful, non-deceptive advertising that does not undermine public confidence in mediation.

---

## Applicable Standards Summary

**2025 Family Standard XIV** — A mediator shall be truthful and not misleading when advertising, and shall not make claims that the mediator is unable to substantiate.

**2005 General Standard VII** — A mediator shall be truthful and not misleading when advertising, soliciting, or otherwise communicating the mediator's qualifications, experience, services, and fees.

| Requirement | Level | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Truthful advertising | SHALL | 2025-XIV, 2005-VII |
| No guaranteed outcomes | SHALL | 2025-XIV, 2005-VII |
| Accurate credentials | SHALL | 2025-XIV, 2005-VII |
| State rule compliance | SHALL | 2025-XIV |

---

## Core Requirements

- A mediator **shall** be truthful in advertising and solicitation for mediation
- Advertising must not be false, misleading, or deceptive
- Must not promise or guarantee specific outcomes
- Must accurately represent qualifications, training, experience, and credentials
- Must not imply judicial or governmental endorsement unless actually granted
- Must comply with applicable state advertising rules for any professional license held (attorney, therapist, etc.)

---

## Advertising Channels Checklist

### Website & Online Presence
- [ ] Qualifications accurately stated (degrees, certifications, training hours)
- [ ] Experience accurately represented (years, case types, number of mediations)
- [ ] Professional memberships accurately listed (current, not expired)
- [ ] Bio and "About" sections reviewed within the last 12 months
- [ ] Fees and fee structures accurately represented (or clearly marked "contact for current rates")
- [ ] No guarantees of outcomes, settlement rates, or party satisfaction
- [ ] No misleading claims about the mediation process
- [ ] Accessibility features present (alt text, readable fonts, mobile-friendly)

### Social Media
- [ ] Profiles consistent with website information
- [ ] Posts do not disclose client identities or case details (even anonymized)
- [ ] No implied endorsements from courts, judges, or other professionals without permission
- [ ] Responses to comments maintain neutrality and do not disparage other mediators
- [ ] Shared content accurately represents mediation (not oversimplified or misleading)

### Directories & Listing Sites
- [ ] Mediate.com, ACR, state bar, and court directory listings are current
- [ ] Certifications and credentials match current status
- [ ] Practice areas accurately reflect actual competence and training
- [ ] Contact information is current

### Print Materials & Presentations
- [ ] Brochures, business cards, and handouts reviewed for accuracy
- [ ] CLE/conference presentation materials accurately represent qualifications
- [ ] No claims of court endorsement unless actually court-appointed/connected

### Client Reviews & Testimonials
- [ ] Testimonials (if used) comply with applicable professional rules
- [ ] No solicitation of reviews during active mediation
- [ ] Responses to negative reviews maintain confidentiality (never disclose case details)
- [ ] Review sites monitored for accuracy of mediator-provided information

---

## Common Violations to Avoid

| Violation | Why It's Problematic |
|---|---|
| "100% success rate" or any guaranteed outcome | Misleading; outcomes depend on party cooperation |
| Listing expired certifications or memberships | Misrepresents current qualifications |
| "Faster, cheaper, and better than court" | Cannot guarantee comparative outcomes |
| "Certified Mediator" without recognized certification | Misleading title; many "certifications" lack standards |
| Implying specialization without corresponding training | Violates competence standards (2025-XI) |
| Testimonials promising specific results | Suggests guaranteed outcomes |
| Using "Judge" or "Former Judge" without context | May imply judicial authority in mediation |
| Claiming court endorsement without appointment | Misrepresents relationship with court system |
| Settlement statistics without context | Misleading without sample size, case types, timeframe |

---

## Review Schedule

Mediators should review all advertising materials:
- **Annually** — full audit of website, directories, print materials, social media profiles
- **Upon credential change** — when certifications expire, memberships lapse, or new training is completed
- **Before launching new marketing** — review all new materials against this checklist
- **After receiving feedback** — if a party, attorney, or colleague flags a concern
