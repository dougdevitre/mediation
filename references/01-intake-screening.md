# 01 — Intake Screening

> Standards: 2025 Family Standards III (Education of Parties), IV (Barriers to Participation); 2005 General Standard VI (Quality of the Process)

## Purpose

The intake process ensures each prospective party understands what mediation is, assesses whether mediation is appropriate for the family, and identifies barriers to meaningful participation — all before seeking consent to mediate.

---

## Applicable Standards Summary

### 2025 Standard III — Education of Parties

The mediator **shall** educate potential parties about the mediation process before it begins, including:

- Mediation is consensual; the mediator is an impartial facilitator who will not make decisions or pressure settlement
- The mediator's style and approach
- How mediation differs from evaluative processes (e.g., parenting plan evaluations)
- Confidentiality obligations and exceptions
- Either party or the mediator may terminate at any time
- Attorney/advocate presence may be required or warranted
- Separate and/or online sessions may be available
- Court approval requirements for agreements
- How to enforce or modify agreements
- Self-represented parties should obtain independent legal advice

### 2025 Standard IV — Barriers to Participation

The mediator **shall**:
- Meet separately and confidentially with each party to examine capacity for self-determination and informed decision-making
- Explore barriers: domestic abuse, child maltreatment, behavioral concerns, substance use, self-representation, language barriers, literacy, cultural norms, financial pressure, technology access/competence
- Assess whether safeguards and process modifications can address barriers
- Not conduct mediation if any party is unable or unwilling to meaningfully participate

## Intake Checklist

### Initial Contact
- [ ] Record referral source (court-ordered, attorney referral, self-referred, agency)
- [ ] Collect basic contact information for each party
- [ ] Confirm case type (divorce, custody, modification, paternity, grandparent, other)
- [ ] Explain that individual pre-mediation sessions will be scheduled separately
- [ ] Provide written overview of mediation process (or direct to informational materials)

### Individual Pre-Mediation Session (each party, separately)
- [ ] Explain mediator's role, style, and approach
- [ ] Explain self-determination: parties make their own decisions
- [ ] Explain confidentiality and its exceptions (mandatory reporting, threats of harm, court order)
- [ ] Explain right to withdraw at any time
- [ ] Explain right to have attorney/advocate present
- [ ] Explain option of separate sessions (caucus/shuttle) and online sessions
- [ ] Explain court approval requirements if applicable
- [ ] Explain how agreements can be enforced or modified
- [ ] Recommend obtaining independent legal advice
- [ ] Conduct domestic abuse screening (→ references/02)
- [ ] Conduct child maltreatment screening (→ references/03)
- [ ] Assess barriers to participation (→ references/10)
- [ ] Assess party's capacity for self-determination and informed decision-making
- [ ] Discuss process modifications that could address identified barriers
- [ ] Document screening results (confidential mediator notes)

### Appropriateness Determination
- [ ] Is mediation safe for both parties?
- [ ] Can identified barriers be adequately addressed through process modifications?
- [ ] Are both parties willing and able to meaningfully participate?
- [ ] If domestic abuse is present, can safeguards ensure autonomous decision-making? (→ references/02)
- [ ] If child maltreatment is present, does mediator have required specialized training? (→ references/03)
- [ ] Has conflict-of-interest check been completed? (→ references/04)

### Decision
- [ ] **PROCEED**: Both parties screened, barriers addressed, consent obtained → prepare Agreement to Mediate
- [ ] **MODIFY**: Proceed with specific accommodations (shuttle mediation, support persons, online format, etc.)
- [ ] **DECLINE/REFER**: Mediation is not appropriate → provide referrals to alternative processes, legal aid, domestic abuse services, or mental health resources

---

## Key Reminders

- **Never skip individual pre-mediation sessions.** The 2025 standards use "shall" — this is mandatory, not optional.
- **Screen every case for domestic abuse.** Even when neither party raises it. Even in court-referred cases. Even in "friendly" divorces.
- **Barrier assessment is ongoing.** New information may emerge at any session. Reassess throughout the mediation.
- **Self-represented parties need extra attention.** Ensure they understand their right to consult an attorney at any point.
- **Cultural humility.** Be aware of how culture, religion, language, immigration status, and socio-economic status may affect participation.
- **Document your process.** Maintain confidential intake notes showing that screening and education were completed.
