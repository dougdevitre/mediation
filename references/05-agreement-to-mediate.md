# 05 — Agreement to Mediate

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard III(B)–(C); 2005 General Standard I (Self-Determination)

## Purpose

Prior to the first mediation session, the mediator **shall** prepare and ask the parties to sign a written agreement to mediate containing the terms and conditions. Additional participants (except children) **should** also sign.

---

## Required Elements

The Agreement to Mediate should include:

### Process Description
- [ ] Definition of mediation and the mediator's role
- [ ] Mediator's style and approach
- [ ] Statement that mediation is voluntary and consensual
- [ ] Statement that the mediator will not make decisions for the parties
- [ ] Statement that the mediator will not pressure settlement

### Self-Determination
- [ ] Parties may withdraw at any time
- [ ] Parties may agree or decline any proposal
- [ ] Parties are encouraged to consult independent attorneys

### Confidentiality
- [ ] Scope of confidentiality protections
- [ ] Exceptions to confidentiality (mandatory reporting, threats of harm, court orders)
- [ ] Confidentiality of separate/caucus sessions
- [ ] Technology and data security provisions
- [ ] Agreement not to subpoena the mediator

### Participants
- [ ] Who may attend sessions (attorneys, advocates, support persons)
- [ ] Conditions for attorney/advocate participation
- [ ] Role of support persons

### Process Logistics
- [ ] Session format (in-person, online, hybrid, shuttle)
- [ ] Session scheduling and cancellation policies
- [ ] Communication between sessions
- [ ] Document sharing procedures

### Fees
- [ ] Fee structure and rates
- [ ] Payment terms and responsibility
- [ ] Cancellation/no-show policy
- [ ] Sliding scale or reduced fee availability (if applicable)

### Legal Status
- [ ] Mediation is not a substitute for legal advice
- [ ] Agreements should be reviewed by independent counsel before signing
- [ ] Court approval requirements (if applicable)
- [ ] How agreements can be enforced or modified

### Termination
- [ ] Mediator's right to suspend or terminate
- [ ] Grounds for termination
- [ ] Process for termination
