# 11 — Confidentiality

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard VIII; 2005 General Standard V

## Purpose

Maintain confidentiality of all information acquired in mediation unless permitted or required to reveal it by law, rule, or agreement of the parties.

---

## Mandatory Requirements

### Explanation (SHALL)
- Explain to participants the meaning and purposes of confidentiality and privilege
- Inform participants of limitations and exceptions

### Exceptions to Confidentiality
- Mandatory reporting of child maltreatment
- Threats of harm (including suicide or violence)
- Court orders
- Statutory exceptions in the applicable jurisdiction
- Party agreement to disclose

### Agreement to Mediate (SHOULD)
- Include confidentiality provisions in the signed agreement
- Address technology and online communication tools
- Address separate/caucus session confidentiality

### Subpoenas (SHALL / SHOULD)
- If subpoenaed, inform the parties immediately
- Shall not testify or produce documents without a court order if doing so would violate confidentiality obligations or jurisdictional law
- This includes digital or electronically stored information

### Data Security (SHALL)
- Implement robust data security measures to protect all digital information from unauthorized access

---

## Confidentiality Scope

| What is confidential | What is NOT confidential |
|---|---|
| All communications during mediation | Information subject to mandatory reporting |
| Information shared in caucus (unless party consents to share) | Information parties agree to disclose |
| Mediator's notes and impressions | Information required by court order |
| Documents created for mediation | Pre-existing documents parties brought to mediation (debatable — check jurisdiction) |
| Proposals and offers | The fact that mediation occurred (varies by jurisdiction) |
| Reasons for termination (generally) | A signed mediation agreement (typically not confidential) |

---

## Caucus / Separate Session Confidentiality

Before holding separate sessions:
- [ ] Discuss with all parties what information from separate sessions will remain confidential
- [ ] Obtain clear understanding: nothing shared in caucus will be disclosed to the other party without explicit permission
- [ ] Confirm this understanding before each separate session
- [ ] Track what each party has authorized for disclosure

---

## Technology-Specific Confidentiality

- Use end-to-end encrypted platforms for video/audio sessions
- Use secure document sharing (not unencrypted email)
- Disable recording unless all parties consent and storage is secure
- Establish clear policies on chat/messaging within platforms
- Address data retention and deletion timelines
- Consider screen-sharing risks (visible tabs, notifications)

---

## Key Jurisdiction Variations

> **Important**: Confidentiality and privilege protections vary significantly by jurisdiction. This section provides general guidance. Mediators MUST know their state's mediation confidentiality statute.

Common variations:
- Some states provide broad mediation privilege; others are narrower
- Some states allow courts to compel mediator testimony in limited circumstances
- Mandatory reporting exceptions are universal but vary in scope
- Some states distinguish between court-connected and private mediation confidentiality
- Some states require written confidentiality agreements; others imply confidentiality by statute
