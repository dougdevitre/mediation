# 10 — Barriers to Participation & Process Modifications

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard IV (Barriers to Participation); 2005 General Standard VI (Quality of the Process)

## Purpose

Identify barriers that may challenge or prevent full participation in mediation and design modifications to ensure accessibility and meaningful engagement.

---

## Barrier Categories (2025 Standards)

The mediator **shall** explore separately and confidentially with each party:

1. **Domestic abuse** → references/02
2. **Child maltreatment** → references/03
3. **Behavioral concerns** (mental health conditions, personality disorders, cognitive impairments)
4. **Substance use** (active addiction, recovery status, impact on decision-making)
5. **Self-representation** (no attorney; limited understanding of legal rights)
6. **Language barriers** (limited English proficiency, deaf/hard of hearing)
7. **Literacy** (limited reading ability, document comprehension challenges)
8. **Cultural norms** (communication styles, gender roles, authority deference, decision-making expectations)
9. **Financial pressure** (inability to afford process, power imbalance due to economic disparity)
10. **Technology access and competence** (no internet, limited device access, unfamiliarity with platforms)

---

## Assessment Protocol

For each barrier identified, assess:

| Question | Answer | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Can the party articulate their own interests? | Yes / With support / No | If "No" → consider whether modifications can address this |
| Can the party say "no" to proposals? | Yes / With support / No | If "No" → assess whether self-determination is possible |
| Can the party understand the information presented? | Yes / With support / No | If "No" → provide accommodations or recommend alternative process |
| Is the party making decisions free from coercion? | Yes / Uncertain / No | If "No" → do not mediate |
| Can modifications adequately address the barrier? | Yes / Partially / No | If "No" → refer to alternative process |

---

## Process Modifications Menu

| Barrier | Possible Modifications |
|---|---|
| Domestic abuse | Shuttle mediation, separate sessions, support persons, online from separate locations, staggered scheduling |
| Behavioral concerns | Shorter sessions, co-mediation, breaks, support person present, therapeutic referral |
| Substance use | Assessment of current sobriety, shorter sessions, co-mediation, referral to treatment |
| Self-representation | Recommend attorney consultation, provide legal information (not advice), ensure understanding of rights, allow time for consultation |
| Language barriers | Qualified interpreter, translated documents, bilingual mediator, simplified language |
| Literacy | Oral explanations, visual aids, read-aloud support, simplified documents |
| Cultural norms | Cultural consultant, adjust communication style, involve culturally appropriate support persons, accommodate religious practices |
| Financial pressure | Sliding scale fees, reduced fee, referral to legal aid or pro bono mediation, address economic power imbalance |
| Technology access | Phone participation option, technology training session, provide device or internet access, in-person alternative |
| Power imbalance (general) | Co-mediation, attorney presence, caucus sessions, structured turn-taking, reality testing |

## Key Reminders

- **The mediator should not conduct a mediation if the mediator believes any party is unable or unwilling to meaningfully participate** (Standard IV(D))
- Barrier assessment is **ongoing** — new barriers may emerge at any point
- **Modifications must be effective** — implementing modifications that don't actually address the barrier is worse than not mediating
- **Document your assessment and modifications** in your confidential case notes
