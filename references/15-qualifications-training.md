# 15 — Mediator Qualifications & Ongoing Education

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard XI (Mediator Qualifications); 2005 General Standard IV (Competence)

## Purpose

Ensure mediators are qualified by education, training, and experience, and maintain ongoing professional development.

---

## Core Competence Requirements

### 2005 General Standard IV — Competence

A mediator shall mediate only when the mediator has the necessary competence to satisfy the reasonable expectations of the parties. Competence includes:
- Knowledge of mediation process and skills
- Subject matter expertise sufficient to the case type
- Training and experience
- Cultural competence
- Ability to recognize the limits of one's own competence

### 2025 Family Standard XI — Qualifications & Ongoing Education

A family mediator **shall** be qualified by education and training to undertake the mediation. Key requirements:
- **Initial training**: Comprehensive mediation training covering family mediation-specific topics
- **Domestic abuse training**: Required before mediating any family case (Standard V(C))
- **Child maltreatment training**: Required before mediating cases with child maltreatment allegations (Standard VI(A))
- **Technology competence**: Training in ODR and digital tools (Standard IX(B))
- **Ongoing education**: Continuing professional development
- **Peer consultation and mentoring**: Commitment to professional growth through consultation with experienced mediators

### Core Family Mediation Training

- [ ] Family systems and dynamics

- [ ] Negotiation theory

- [ ] Domestic abuse: nature, context, dynamics, including coercive control
- [ ] Impact of domestic abuse on parenting, co-parenting, and children
- [ ] Impact of domestic abuse on the mediation process
- [ ] Screening for domestic abuse

---

## Professional Development Practices

- **Peer consultation**: Regular case consultation with experienced mediators
- **Mentoring**: Both seeking mentorship and providing it to newer mediators
- **Supervision**: Case supervision (especially for newer mediators)
- **Self-assessment**: Regular reflection on biases, blind spots, and skill gaps
- **Feedback**: Soliciting and incorporating feedback from parties, attorneys, and co-mediators
- **Professional memberships**: Active participation in AFCC, ACR, APFM, or state mediation organizations

---

## Competence Boundaries

A mediator should:
- Recognize when a case requires expertise they do not have
- Decline or withdraw from cases beyond their competence
- Refer to more experienced mediators when appropriate
- Seek co-mediation with a specialist when needed
- Not represent competence in areas where they lack training
