# 12 — Agreement Drafting & Review

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard II(D); 2005 General Standard I (Self-Determination)

## Purpose

Guide the mediator in documenting parties' voluntary resolution and ensuring informed consent to the agreement terms.

---

## Core Requirements

- The mediator **should** document the parties' voluntary resolution
- The mediator **should** inform parties that an independent attorney should review any agreement before signing
- The mediator **should** offer parties time to reflect before signing
- The mediator **shall never** pressure parties into settlement

---

## Agreement Drafting Checklist

### Before Drafting
- [ ] Confirm all agreed terms orally with both parties
- [ ] Ensure both parties understand each term
- [ ] Confirm decisions were voluntary and informed
- [ ] Identify any terms that require independent verification (property values, income figures)

### Agreement Content
- [ ] Parties' names and identifying information
- [ ] Date and reference to mediation sessions
- [ ] Statement that the agreement was reached voluntarily
- [ ] Statement that parties were advised to seek independent legal counsel
- [ ] All substantive terms (custody, parenting plan, property, support, etc.)
- [ ] Implementation timelines
- [ ] Dispute resolution process for future disagreements
- [ ] Modification provisions
- [ ] Signature lines for all parties

### Before Signing
- [ ] Offer each party time to reflect (cooling-off period)
- [ ] Recommend independent attorney review
- [ ] Confirm each party understands the legal effect of signing
- [ ] Confirm court approval requirements (if applicable)

### After Signing
- [ ] Provide copies to all parties
- [ ] Explain next steps (filing with court, attorney review, implementation)
- [ ] Explain how to enforce or modify the agreement

---

## What the Mediator Should NOT Do

- Draft an agreement that constitutes legal advice
- Include terms that one party does not understand
- Rush signing to conclude the mediation
- Include terms that were not discussed and agreed upon
- Draft an agreement when one party appears to be agreeing under duress or pressure
- Represent that the agreement is legally binding without attorney review
