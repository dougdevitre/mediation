# 14 — Fees

> Standards: 2025 Family Standard XIII; 2005 General Standard VIII (Fees & Other Charges)

## Purpose

Ensure transparent, fair fee practices that do not undermine impartiality or create barriers to access.

---

## Core Requirements

### Disclosure (SHALL / SHOULD)
- Provide complete information about fees **before** mediation begins
- Include fee terms in the Agreement to Mediate
- Disclose fee structure, rates, payment terms, and cancellation policies
- Disclose any sliding scale or reduced fee availability

### Prohibited Practices
- **No contingent fees**: A mediator shall not charge fees contingent upon the result of the mediation or the amount of the settlement
- **No outcome-based fees**: The mediator must not have a financial interest in the outcome
- **No pressure from fees**: Fee arrangements should not pressure parties toward settlement or continuation

### Impartiality and Fees
- A mediator may accept unequal fee payments from the parties
- However, unequal payment must not adversely impact the mediator's impartiality
- If one party is paying more, the mediator must remain vigilant about potential bias toward the paying party

---

## Fee Disclosure Checklist

- [ ] Hourly rate or flat fee per session
- [ ] Retainer requirements
- [ ] Payment schedule (per session, monthly, etc.)
- [ ] Who pays (split equally, one party, proportional to income)
- [ ] Cancellation and no-show fees
- [ ] Charges for preparation time, document review, drafting
- [ ] Charges for phone calls, emails between sessions
- [ ] Sliding scale availability and criteria
- [ ] Pro bono or reduced-fee programs
- [ ] Payment methods accepted
- [ ] Consequences of non-payment
- [ ] Refund policy

---

## Access Considerations

- Financial barriers are explicitly listed in the 2025 standards as a barrier to participation (Standard IV)
- The mediator should consider whether fee arrangements create a power imbalance
- When one party cannot afford mediation, explore: sliding scale, legal aid mediation programs, court-connected free mediation, community mediation centers, pro bono panels
